# Cover Letter Formula — What Actually Gets Read

Four sentences. Nothing else.

Sentence one: Name the specific problem their company faces right now. Mirror language from the job posting. Prove you did the work.

Sentences two and three: One specific result you produced that proves you can solve their problem. Use a real number — before and after. Not responsibilities. Results.

Sentence four: Exactly why this company, not just this role. Reference something specific about their product or recent news.

Close: "Would you have 15 minutes next week?" Direct. Easy to say yes to.

The mistake: writing about yourself. Write about their problem and prove you've solved something like it.
